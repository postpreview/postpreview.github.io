Best Practices

10 Tips for High-Performing Tweets

Writing better tweets starts with understanding what actually works in the X feed in 2025.

1
Lead with the hook in the first line
The first line is all that's visible before "Show more." Make it a bold statement, a surprising fact, or a direct question. If it doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.
2
Keep links out of the main tweet
X's algorithm reduces the distribution of tweets with external links. Post the link in a reply to your own tweet, then quote-tweet that reply to drive clicks without the reach penalty.
3
Use 1–2 hashtags maximum
Tweets with 1–2 targeted hashtags get up to 21% more engagement than those with 3+. Each hashtag eats into your 280 characters — choose only the most relevant ones.
4
Post between 9am–11am on weekdays
Tuesday and Thursday mornings consistently drive the highest engagement on X. Professionals check feeds before starting work. Avoid early mornings and weekend evenings.
5
Add images to boost visibility
Tweets with images get 150% more retweets than text-only posts. Use the 16:9 ratio (1600×900 px) and keep important elements centred — Twitter crops the sides on mobile.
6
Reply to your own tweet within the first hour
Early engagement signals content quality to the algorithm. Drop a follow-up thought, a relevant link, or invite replies — this creates a mini thread that keeps people in your tweet longer.
7
Use line breaks for readability
Dense walls of text get skipped. Break your tweet into 1–2 line chunks with breathing room. White space is your friend in a fast-scrolling feed, especially on mobile screens.
8
Ask a direct question to drive replies
Questions ending with "What do you think?" or "Have you tried this?" consistently generate more replies. Replies push your tweet into the feeds of the replier's followers too.
9
Avoid deleting and reposting
Deleting a tweet and reposting resets all engagement metrics to zero and removes any reshares. If you spot an error, a quote-tweet with the correction often performs better anyway.
10
Preview before you post
Always use a preview tool to check how your tweet looks in the actual feed — especially for image cropping, hashtag formatting, and how the first line reads before the "Show more" cut-off.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Twitter/X previews, character limits, and posting best practices.

What is the Twitter / X character limit in 2025? ▾
Standard Twitter/X accounts have a 280-character limit per tweet. X Premium subscribers can post up to 25,000 characters in long-form posts. Importantly, all URLs count as exactly 23 characters regardless of their actual length, and images attached to tweets do not count toward the character limit. See our full Character Limits Guide for all platform limits.
What is the best image size for a Twitter / X post? ▾
The recommended in-stream image size for Twitter/X is 1600 × 900 px (16:9 ratio). Twitter crops images to this ratio in the feed, so place your main subject in the centre of the frame. For other image types: profile photo 400 × 400 px (displayed circular), header/banner 1500 × 500 px (3:1 ratio), and link card images 800 × 418 px. See our Image Size Guide for the full breakdown.
How many hashtags should I use on Twitter / X? ▾
X recommends using 1–2 hashtags per tweet. Research shows tweets with 1–2 hashtags get up to 21% more engagement than those with 3 or more. Each hashtag also eats into your 280-character limit, so choose only the most relevant ones. Hashtags work best on X for joining real-time trending conversations, industry events, and live conferences. Read our complete Hashtag Guide 2025 for the full strategy.

What's the difference between the Blue and Gold verified badge? ▾
On X (Twitter), the Blue checkmark is given to individual X Premium subscribers who pay for the subscription. The Gold checkmark is awarded to verified organisations and businesses. Our preview tool lets you simulate both badge types so you can see how your profile line appears alongside the tweet. There is also a Grey badge for government accounts.
Can I preview how a Twitter thread looks? ▾
The current tool previews individual tweets with full character counting, image preview, and profile simulation. Thread-specific previewing is on our roadmap. In the meantime, use the tool to preview each tweet in your thread individually — this lets you catch character overages, hashtag formatting issues, and image crops on a per-tweet basis before threading them together.
Does attaching an image count toward the 280-character limit? ▾
No. Images, GIFs, and videos attached to tweets do not count toward the 280-character limit on X. Only text characters and URLs count. URLs are always counted as 23 characters regardless of how long or short they actually are, so using a URL shortener doesn't save you any characters on Twitter/X.
What is the best time to post on Twitter / X in 2025? ▾
Based on 2025 data, the best times to post on Twitter/X are Tuesday and Thursday, 9am–11am in your audience's local timezone. Weekday mornings drive the highest engagement as professionals check feeds before starting work. For entertainment or consumer brands, evenings (7pm–9pm) also perform well. Always check your own analytics to find when your specific audience is most active. See our Social Media Tips Guide for full posting schedule recommendations.
